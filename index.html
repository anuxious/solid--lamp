About

Hello! I am Anoushka. I'm 15 years old and I'm in 11th grade studying STEM subjects. I'm a Kathak Dancer and have been dancing since I was 3 and have a national level diploma in Kathak Dance.

I'm also a singer and have been doing Indian Classical Music for more than have a decade now. I can also play the harmonium and keyboard along with a little bit of tabla.

Additionally, I'm a Full Stack Developer who has a strong backbone in Front End Web Development. I'm also a beginner at App Development and Ethical Hacking. I've also been learning Artificial Intelligence for a few years now and I'm really interested in pursuing it along with Computer Engineering in the near future.

I'm also a Graphic Designer who is worked in Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator. I also enjoy creating small hand drawn designs on Procreate and own a few POD businesses to sell these designs in forms of articles like apparel, accessories etc.

I'd say I genuinely enjoy helping others a lot and am a feminist who advocates for Mental Health especially in Children and young adults. I'm the founder of the Non Profit Organisations: Theraprize and Groceree and I also conduct various fundraiser events and campaigns on a very regular basis.

The Survival Game

With detailed graphics (simple graphics available on block version) and easy to use controls, this is a simple avoid coming in contact with the lava game and once you collect all the coins of that level, you're automatically ascended to the next level. You're given three lives for each level which get eventually harder and harder and your goal is to not fall in the lava while collecting all the coins that come across your way. After you've used up all your lives, you lose and are asked to exit or replay.

This game is a great way to improve your problem solving and goal orientation skills since you're made to focus on the task and only by playing with full concentration will you be able to solve the problem (i.e. avoid the lava) and achieve your goal (i.e collecting all the coins) and finally make it to the end. Happy Playing !

Non Profit Organisations

Theraprize

In India, alonsgide with the cost of therapy being so excessively high, public stigma is a factor in the underreported prevalence of mental disorders, with only 7.3% of young people in India reporting a mental disorder and fewer accessing treatment.
Compared to adults, young people do not seek help for mental health problems due to financial restraints, peer pressure, a desire to be self-reliant, lack of knowledge to recognize mental health problems or lack of awareness about mental-health-related services.

Our goal is to connect students and young adults to free/affordable mental health professionals
